**Purpose**: Keep users informed, attract new users

**Content Types**:
- New feature announcements
- App update releases
- Bug fix communications
- Upcoming feature previews
- User-requested feature completions

**Format**:
```

[Feature 1 with benefit]
[Feature 2 with benefit]
[Feature 3 with benefit]

Update now: [link]
```

#### Pillar 2: Industry News & Commentary (25%)
**Purpose**: Position as informed industry participant

**Content Types**:
- AHA/guideline update coverage
- EMS industry news commentary
- Research study highlights
- Policy development discussion
- Conference live coverage

**Approach**:
- Share news with added insight
- Quote-tweet with valuable context
- Thread format for complex topics
- Always cite sources

#### Pillar 3: Educational Value (25%)
**Purpose**: Provide ongoing value to followers

**Content Types**:
- Protocol tips and reminders
- Clinical pearls (brief format)
- "Did you know" facts
- Infographic shares
- Quick reference content

**Format Examples**:
```
Quick protocol reminder:

Adenosine for SVT:
First dose: 6mg rapid IV push
Second dose: 12mg if no conversion
Third dose: 12mg

Always follow your local protocols.

#EMSEducation
```

#### Pillar 4: Community Engagement (25%)
**Purpose**: Build relationships and brand affinity

**Content Types**:
- User testimonials (with permission)
- EMS celebrations (EMS Week, milestones)
- Community conversations
- Support responses
- User-generated content amplification

### Tweet Formats

#### Single Tweets
- Maximum 280 characters
- One key message per tweet
- Visual when possible
- Clear CTA or engagement prompt

#### Threads
- Use for complex topics
- Number tweets (1/7, 2/7, etc.)
- First tweet must hook
- Last tweet = summary + CTA
- 5-10 tweets optimal

#### Quote Tweets
- Add genuine value
- Provide context or insight
- Respectful disagreement when warranted
- Amplify community voices

#### Media Tweets
- Screenshots (app features)
- Infographics (protocols)
- Short video clips (<2:20)
- GIFs for personality (sparingly)

## Compliance Considerations

### Medical Information Standards

1. **Accuracy Requirements**
   - All clinical content reviewed before posting
   - Sources cited for medical claims
   - "Follow your local protocols" disclaimers
   - No patient-specific advice

2. **Scope Limitations**
   - Educational/reference content only
   - No diagnostic suggestions
   - Acknowledge jurisdictional variations
   - Defer to medical direction

3. **Disclaimer Usage**
   For clinical threads:
   ```
   Disclaimer: General educational information.
   Protocols vary by region. Always follow your
   local protocols and medical direction.
   ```

### Platform Compliance

1. **Twitter/X Rules**
   - No misleading health information
   - Accurate product representations
   - No engagement manipulation
   - Authentic account behavior

2. **FTC Guidelines**
   - Disclose paid partnerships
   - Clear sponsored content marking
   - Honest product claims
   - Influencer relationship disclosure

### Brand Safety

1. **Content Boundaries**
   - No political content
   - No controversial EMS debates
   - No disparaging competitors
   - No workplace drama engagement

2. **Crisis Protocol**
   - Pause scheduled content during tragedies
   - Supportive messaging only during crises
   - No marketing during sensitive periods
   - Leadership approval for crisis response

3. **Competitor Engagement**
   - Never negative about competitors
   - Don't engage in comparison battles
   - Focus on Protocol Guide strengths
   - Acknowledge when alternatives suit user better

### HIPAA & Privacy

1. **User Content**
   - Never share identifiable patient info (even if user does)
   - Redirect HIPAA-violating content discussions to DM
   - Report obvious HIPAA violations if severe

2. **User Privacy**
   - Don't share user support issues publicly without consent
   - Anonymize testimonials or get permission
   - Protect user account information

## Engagement Strategy

### Daily Engagement Tasks

**Morning (9 AM)**:
- Review overnight mentions
- Respond to any support requests
- Check industry news for commentary opportunities
- Engage with 5-10 relevant tweets

**Midday (12 PM)**:
- Post scheduled content
- Engage with responses
- Quote-tweet valuable community content
- Support ticket follow-ups

**Afternoon (3 PM)**:
- Check trending EMS topics
- Engage in active conversations
- Respond to new mentions
- Identify content opportunities

**Evening (6 PM)**:
- Final mention check
- Schedule next day content
- Wrap up support threads
- Engagement with late posts

### Engagement Priorities

1. **Support requests** - Respond within 1 hour
2. **Direct questions** - Respond within 2 hours
3. **Positive mentions** - Like + respond within 4 hours
4. **Industry discussions** - Engage same day
5. **General mentions** - Engage within 24 hours

### Conversation Guidelines

**Tone**: Professional but personable
**Voice**: Knowledgeable peer, not corporate speak
**Personality**: Helpful, humble, occasionally witty

**Do**:
- Use "we" for company matters
- Admit mistakes openly
- Thank users for feedback
- Celebrate community wins

**Don't**:
- Be defensive
- Argue with critics
- Use corporate jargon
- Ignore negative feedback
