## Tips for Better Voice Recognition

### Speak Clearly

- Use a **normal speaking pace** â€“ not too fast, not too slow
- **Pronounce medical terms** carefully
- **Minimize background noise** when possible
- **Hold the device** 6-12 inches from your mouth

### Handling Noisy Environments

Ambulance sirens, radio chatter, and scene noise can interfere. Try:

1. **Cup your hand** around the microphone
2. **Step away** from noise sources if safe
3. **Speak louder** than normal conversation
4. **Use short, clear phrases**

### Medical Terminology

The voice recognition is trained on medical terms. It understands:

- Drug names: "Epinephrine," "Adenosine," "Amiodarone"
- Conditions: "STEMI," "CVA," "Anaphylaxis"
- Abbreviations: "SVT," "VF," "AMS," "SOB"
- Procedures: "Intubation," "Cardioversion," "IO access"

## Troubleshooting Voice Search

### "Voice search isn't working"

**Check microphone permissions:**

*iPhone/iPad:*
1. Go to **Settings** â†’ **Safari** (or your browser)
2. Find **Microphone** â†’ Enable for protocol-guide.com

*Android:*
1. Go to **Settings** â†’ **Apps** â†’ **Chrome** (or your browser)
2. Find **Permissions** â†’ **Microphone** â†’ Allow

*Desktop:*
1. Look for the microphone icon in the address bar
2. Click and select **Allow**

### "It's not understanding me"

- Speak more slowly and clearly
- Reduce background noise
- Check microphone hardware
- Try typing the query instead

### "Transcription is incorrect"

- Medical terms may need careful pronunciation
- Edit the transcription before searching
- Use the text correction feature

### "Voice search is slow"

- Requires internet connection
- Check your network signal
- Processing takes 1-2 seconds
