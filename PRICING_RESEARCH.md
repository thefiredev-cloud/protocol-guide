## Recommended Pricing Tiers

### Per-User Annual Pricing (Preferred for Government)

| Agency Size | Monthly/User | Annual/User | Rationale |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|-----------|
| Small (<50 users) | $8.33 | **$100** | Higher touch, more value per user |
| Mid (50-500) | $6.25 | **$75** | Sweet spot for scaling |
| Large (500-2000) | $5.00 | **$60** | Volume discount, still premium |
| Enterprise (2000+) | $4.17 | **$50** | LA County tier, competitive |

### Annual vs Monthly

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| Government procurement | **Annual strongly preferred** (fiscal year budgets) |
| Discount for annual | **20%** (industry standard, "2 months free") |
| Contract length | **3-year with 25% discount** for lock-in |
| Renewal | Auto-renew with 3% annual increase |

## Critical Pricing Insight

**Your LA County proposal sets the anchor.**

If you propose $31/user to LA County, every other agency will ask "why are we paying more?"

**Recommendation:**
- Launch at $40-50/user for standard pricing
- Offer "strategic partnership rates" ($30-35) for reference customers like LA County
- Build to premium pricing ($60-75) only after establishing market position

## Value Proposition Framing

**Don't sell price. Sell value:**

> "Protocol-Guide costs less than **$0.50 per EMS call** — and saves 30 seconds per patient contact. For a department running 180,000 calls per year, that's **3,750 hours saved** annually."

**ROI calculation:**
- 3,750 hours saved × $75/hour (fully-burdened paramedic cost) = $281,250 value
