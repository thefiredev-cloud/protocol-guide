# Protocol Guide Interview Guide

## Interview Overview

**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Format**: Remote video call or in-person
**Recording**: Audio only (with consent)
**Note-taker**: Required for all sessions

---

## Pre-Interview Checklist

- [ ] Confirm participant meets screening criteria (active EMT/Paramedic)
- [ ] Send calendar invite with video link
- [ ] Prepare consent form for signature
- [ ] Test recording equipment
- [ ] Have backup questions ready
- [ ] Note participant segment (New EMT / Experienced / Rural / Urban)

---

## Introduction Script (5 minutes)

> Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. My name is [NAME] and I'm researching how EMS professionals access medical protocols in the field.

> This interview will take about 45-60 minutes. There are no right or wrong answers - I'm genuinely interested in your experiences and honest opinions. Everything you share will be kept confidential and anonymized in our reports.

> Before we start, I need to cover a few things:
> - With your permission, I'd like to record this conversation for note-taking purposes. The recording will not be shared outside our research team.
> - You can skip any question you're not comfortable answering.
> - You can stop the interview at any time.
> - Do I have your permission to record? [WAIT FOR VERBAL CONSENT]

> Do you have any questions before we begin?

---

## Section 1: Background and Context (10 minutes)

### Warmup Questions

1. **Tell me about yourself and your role in EMS.**
   - How long have you been in EMS?
   - What's your current certification level (EMT-B, AEMT, Paramedic)?
   - What type of service do you work for? (Fire-based, private, hospital, volunteer)
   - Urban, suburban, or rural environment?

2. **Describe a typical shift for you.**
   - How many hours?
   - Average call volume?
   - What types of calls do you see most often?

3. **What devices do you carry or have access to on the rig?**
   - Personal phone?
   - Agency-issued tablet?
   - MDT/MDC (Mobile Data Terminal)?
   - What's your cellular coverage like in your response area?

---

## Section 2: Current Protocol Access (15 minutes)

### Understanding Current Workflow

4. **Walk me through how you currently access protocols.**
   - What resources do you use? (PDF, binder, app, memory)
   - Where are these resources physically located?
   - Do you access them before, during, or after calls?

5. **Tell me about the last time you needed to look up a protocol on a call.**
   - What was the situation?
   - What did you do to find the information?
   - How long did it take?
   - Were you able to find what you needed?

6. **When you're in the back of the ambulance treating a patient, how do you access protocol information?**
   - Do you ever look things up while working on a patient?
   - What makes that difficult?
   - [PROBE: gloves, lighting, bouncing, time pressure]

7. **How do protocols get updated at your agency?**
   - How do you find out about changes?
   - What happens to old printed protocols?
   - Have you ever used an outdated protocol by mistake?

### Pain Point Exploration

8. **What frustrates you most about how you currently access protocols?**
   - [Let them answer first, then probe if needed]
   - PROBES:
     - Speed of finding information?
     - Accuracy/currency of information?
     - Physical handling issues?
     - Search/navigation?

9. **Have you ever NOT looked something up when you probably should have? Why?**
   - What prevented you from looking it up?
   - How did you feel about that decision?

10. **If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about protocol access, what would it be?**

---

## Section 3: Physical Environment Challenges (10 minutes)

### Field Conditions Deep Dive

11. **Let's talk about wearing gloves. How does that affect your ability to use phones or tablets?**
    - What type of gloves do you typically wear?
    - Have you found any workarounds?
    - Do you ever remove gloves to use a device?

12. **How does the ambulance environment affect protocol access?**
    - Lighting in the patient compartment
    - Motion/bouncing while moving
    - Noise level
    - Space constraints

13. **Think about a high-stress call - cardiac arrest, pediatric emergency, major trauma. Would you realistically be able to look up a protocol during that call?**
    - Why or why not?
    - What would need to change?

14. **How do you handle protocol access when you have poor or no cell service?**
    - How often does this happen?
    - What do you do when it happens?

---

## Section 4: Feature Exploration (10 minutes)

### Voice Search

15. **How would you feel about using voice commands to search for protocols?**
    - "Hey Protocol Guide, what's the epi dose for anaphylaxis?"
    - Would you use this on scene? In the rig?
    - What concerns would you have?
    - [PROBE: privacy, noise, accuracy]

### Search and Organization

16. **When you need to find a protocol, do you know exactly what you're looking for, or do you need to browse/explore?**
    - Give me an example of each scenario
    - How do you currently handle the "I don't know exactly what I need" situation?

17. **What search terms would you naturally use to find a cardiac arrest protocol?**
    - [Note: Are they using medical terminology or plain language?]
    - How about pediatric respiratory distress?
    - Overdose reversal?

### Medication Calculators

18. **How do you currently calculate weight-based medication doses?**
    - Do you use any apps or tools?
    - Which medications do you calculate most often?
    - Pediatric vs. adult?
    - What's your biggest concern with medication calculations?

### Offline Access

19. **How important is it that a protocol app works without internet connection?**
    - On a scale of 1-10, how critical is offline functionality?
    - Would you trust an app that ONLY works offline? (No live updates)

---

## Section 5: Current Solutions (5 minutes)

### Competitive Landscape

20. **What apps or digital tools, if any, do you currently use for EMS reference?**
    - [Let them list freely]
    - COMMON ONES TO PROBE IF NOT MENTIONED:
      - Paramedicine 101
      - Medscape
      - Epocrates
      - Agency-specific apps
      - State protocol PDFs

21. **For each tool mentioned: What do you like about it? What's missing?**

22. **If you don't use any digital tools, why not?**
    - Have you tried any in the past?
    - What would convince you to try one?

---

## Section 6: Ideal Solution (5 minutes)

### Visioning

23. **Describe your ideal protocol reference tool. Don't worry about what's possible - just describe what would be perfect for you.**
    - How would you access it?
    - How would you find information?
    - What information would it contain?

24. **What would make you stop using your current method and switch to a new app?**
    - What's the minimum it would need to do?
    - What would make it a "must have" vs. "nice to have"?

25. **How much would you pay for a protocol app that solved all your problems?**
    - Monthly subscription?
    - One-time purchase?
    - Would your agency pay for it?

---

## Section 7: Closing (5 minutes)

26. **Is there anything about protocol access or EMS reference tools that I haven't asked about but should know?**

27. **Do you know other EMS professionals who might be interested in participating in this research?**
    - [Snowball recruitment]

28. **Would you be interested in testing a new protocol app and providing feedback in a few weeks?**
    - [Recruit for usability testing]

> Thank you so much for your time today. Your insights are incredibly valuable and will directly influence how we build tools for EMS professionals.

> As a thank you, we'll send you a $75 Amazon gift card within 5 business days. Is [EMAIL] still the best place to send that?

> Do you have any final questions for me?

## Adaptation Notes

### For New EMTs (0-2 years)
- Emphasize questions about learning and confidence building
- Ask more about training vs. field protocol use
- Explore relationship with preceptors/FTOs

### For Experienced Paramedics (5+ years)
- Focus on edge cases and rare protocols
- Ask about teaching and mentoring use cases
- Explore how experience affects protocol reliance

### For Rural EMS
- Spend more time on connectivity challenges
- Ask about extended transport times and protocol decisions
- Explore volunteer vs. career dynamics if relevant

### For Urban High-Volume
- Focus on speed and efficiency
- Ask about call interruption scenarios
- Explore how protocol use varies by call type
