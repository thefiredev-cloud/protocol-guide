# EMS Protocol Research Notes

## Cardiac Arrest Algorithm (ACLS)

### Shockable Rhythms (VF/pVT)
1. Start CPR - 100-120 compressions/min, 30:2 ratio if no advanced airway
2. Attach monitor/defibrillator
3. Check rhythm (pause <10 seconds)
4. If shockable: Shock 120-200 joules
5. CPR 2 minutes, establish IV access
6. Epinephrine 1mg IV/IO every 3-5 minutes
7. Consider advanced airway, 1 breath every 6 seconds
8. Amiodarone 300mg IV/IO first dose, 150mg second dose for refractory VF/pVT
9. Treat reversible causes (Hs and Ts)

### Non-Shockable Rhythms (Asystole/PEA)
1. Continue CPR
2. Establish IV access
3. Epinephrine 1mg IV/IO every 3-5 minutes
4. Consider advanced airway
5. Treat reversible causes

### Hs and Ts (Reversible Causes)
- Hypovolemia
- Hypoxia
- Hydrogen ions (acidosis)
- Hypo/Hyperkalemia
- Hypothermia
- Tension pneumothorax
- Tamponade (cardiac)
- Toxins
- Thrombosis (pulmonary/coronary)

## Medications Reference
- Epinephrine: 1mg IV/IO every 3-5 minutes
- Amiodarone: 300mg IV/IO first dose, 150mg IV/IO second dose
- Lidocaine: 1-1.5 mg/kg IV/IO first dose, 0.5-0.75 mg/kg subsequent doses


## Stroke Protocol (FAST Assessment)

### FAST Assessment
- **F - Face Drooping**: Ask person to smile, check for one-sided droop
- **A - Arm Weakness**: Ask to raise both arms, check for drift
- **S - Speech Difficulty**: Ask to repeat sentence, check for slurring
- **T - Time**: Document symptom onset time, call 911 immediately

### Additional Stroke Symptoms
- Sudden confusion or difficulty understanding speech
- Severe headache with no known cause
- Dizziness, loss of balance, trouble walking
- Sudden vision loss in one or both eyes
- Numbness or weakness in legs

### Prehospital Stroke Management
1. Perform FAST assessment and document findings
2. Establish time of symptom onset (critical for tPA eligibility)
3. Check blood glucose to rule out hypoglycemia
4. Monitor vital signs and oxygen levels
5. Alert nearest stroke center (PSC or CSC)
6. Transport to appropriate stroke-ready facility

### Stroke Types
- **Ischemic Stroke**: Blockage in blood vessel (most common, ~87%)
- **Hemorrhagic Stroke**: Ruptured blood vessel

### Time Windows
- tPA (thrombolytic therapy): Within 3-4.5 hours of symptom onset
- Mechanical thrombectomy: Up to 24 hours in select patients


## Respiratory Distress Protocol

### Assessment
- Mild: Mild wheezing, shortness of breath, cough
- Moderate-Severe: Cyanosis, accessory muscle use, inability to speak full sentences

### Treatment - Asthma/COPD
1. Oxygen to maintain SpO2 > 94%
2. Albuterol 2.5mg nebulized (may repeat x3)
3. Ipratropium 0.5mg nebulized (may combine with albuterol)
4. CPAP for moderate-severe distress (start 5 cmH2O, max 15 cmH2O)
5. Epinephrine 0.3mg IM (1:1000) for severe distress/impending arrest

### CPAP Indications
- CHF/Pulmonary edema
- COPD exacerbation
- Asthma (adjunct)
- Pneumonia

### CPAP Contraindications
- Hypotension
- Suspected pneumothorax
- Upper airway obstruction
- Decreased level of consciousness
- Inability to protect airway

## Opioid Overdose Protocol (Naloxone)

### Signs of Opioid Overdose
- Unresponsive or minimally responsive
- Slow, shallow breathing or no breathing
- Pinpoint pupils
- Cyanosis (blue lips/fingertips)

### Naloxone Administration
1. Intranasal: 4mg (one spray in one nostril)
2. IM/SC: 0.4-2mg 
3. IV: 0.4-2mg (titrate to respiratory effort)
4. May repeat every 2-3 minutes if no response
5. Maximum total dose: 10-12mg (if no response, consider other causes)

### Post-Naloxone Care
- Monitor for re-sedation (naloxone duration: 30-90 min)
- Opioid duration may exceed naloxone duration
- Transport all patients for observation

## Anaphylaxis Protocol

### Signs/Symptoms
- Urticaria (hives), angioedema
- Respiratory distress, wheezing, stridor
- Hypotension, tachycardia
- GI symptoms (nausea, vomiting, cramping)

### Treatment
1. Remove allergen if possible
2. Epinephrine IM (anterolateral thigh):
   - Adult: 0.3-0.5mg (1:1000)
   - Pediatric: 0.01mg/kg (max 0.3mg)
   - May repeat every 5-15 minutes
3. Oxygen to maintain SpO2 > 94%
4. IV fluid bolus for hypotension
5. Diphenhydramine 25-50mg IV/IM (adult)
6. Albuterol for bronchospasm

## Pediatric Dosing Reference

### Epinephrine (Anaphylaxis) - IM 1:1000
- <5kg: 0.01mg/kg
- 5-15kg: 0.1mg (auto-injector available)
- 15-30kg: 0.15mg (EpiPen Jr)
- >30kg: 0.3mg (EpiPen)

### Epinephrine (Cardiac Arrest) - IV/IO 1:10,000
- 0.01mg/kg (0.1mL/kg) every 3-5 minutes

### Albuterol (Nebulized)
- <20kg: 2.5mg
- >20kg: 5mg

### Naloxone
- 0.1mg/kg IV/IO/IM (max 2mg per dose)


## Trauma Protocol

### Primary Survey (ABCDE)
- **A - Airway**: Assess patency, maintain c-spine precautions
- **B - Breathing**: Rate, depth, breath sounds, SpO2
- **C - Circulation**: Pulse, skin color/temp, bleeding control
- **D - Disability**: GCS, pupils, motor function
- **E - Exposure**: Full body exam, temperature management

### Spinal Motion Restriction Indications
- Mechanism of injury suggesting spinal trauma
- Altered mental status
- Neurological deficit
- Spinal pain or tenderness
- Distracting injury

### Hemorrhage Control (Priority Order)
1. Direct pressure
2. Tourniquet (extremity hemorrhage, 2-3 inches proximal to wound)
3. Wound packing with hemostatic agents
4. Pressure dressings

### Tourniquet Guidelines
- Apply 2-3 inches above wound, not over joint
- Tighten until bleeding stops and distal pulse eliminated
- Note time of application
- Do not loosen once applied without physician approval
- Provide analgesia for tourniquet pain

## Diabetic Emergency Protocol

### Hypoglycemia (Blood Glucose <60 mg/dL)

#### Conscious Patient
- Oral glucose 15-30g (glucose gel, juice, candy)
- Recheck in 15 minutes

#### Unconscious/Unable to Swallow
1. Dextrose 50% (D50): 25g IV push (50mL)
   - Pediatric: D10 or D25 preferred
   - D10: 2-5 mL/kg IV
2. Glucagon 1mg IM/SC (if no IV access)
   - Pediatric: 0.5mg if <20kg
3. Recheck glucose every 5-10 minutes
4. May repeat dextrose if glucose remains <60

### Hyperglycemia/DKA
- Fluid resuscitation: NS 20mL/kg bolus
- Monitor for signs of cerebral edema (pediatric)
- Transport for insulin therapy (hospital only)

## Seizure Protocol

### Active Seizure Management
1. Protect patient from injury
2. Suction as needed
3. Oxygen, monitor SpO2
4. Establish IV access when safe

### Benzodiazepine Therapy (Status Epilepticus)
**First-line options:**
- Midazolam 10mg IM (preferred) or 0.2mg/kg IN (max 10mg)
- Midazolam 0.1mg/kg IV/IO (max 4mg)
- Lorazepam 4mg IV
- Diazepam 5-10mg IV or 0.2mg/kg PR (max 10mg)

**Pediatric Dosing:**
- Midazolam 0.2mg/kg IM/IN (max 10mg)
- Midazolam 0.1mg/kg IV/IO (max 4mg)
- Diazepam 0.2mg/kg PR (max 10mg)

### Post-Seizure Care
- Recovery position
- Check blood glucose
- Assess for injuries
- Monitor for recurrence
